ABOUT

Hello, my name is Onur Akın Akbal. I graduated from Dr. Kemal Naci Ekşi Anatolian High School in 2019. I am currently continuing my education life as a senior student in the Department of Chemistry at Yıldız Technical University Davutpaşa Campus. During this time, I had the chance to take part in many teams, including YTU Astronomy Club, YTU Space Physics Research Group, YTU Eren Research Group and Aksuvital Quality-Control Team. At YTU Astronomy Club, I took part in HORIZON Project, KATAY team and other coordinatorships. In these period, I gained valuable experience on the construction of mirror telescopes and earthquake research. After the KATAY project, I participated in Space Physics Research and participated in various scientific activities. During my thesis process, I studied the functionalization and antibacterial properties of chitosan biopolymer in Eren Research Group. I am still continuing my studies in these two teams. In addition, last summer, I completed my compulsory internship as a chemistry intern in the quality control team at Aksuvital. During this internship, I had the opportunity to work with instrumental tools such as HPLC, FTIR, GC, UV and AAS and gained new valuable knowledge about GMP and pharmacopoeia. In the upcoming periods, I aim to take new steps to improve myself in my career, especially in the field of chemistry after graduation. My working interests are polymers, quality-control, software, and my hobbies are amateur astronomy, physics, biology, PC games, TCG card games, cooking and learning new things.

KATAY

In the KATAY Project, which aims to identify the factors causing ionospheric anomalies associated with large earthquakes between 2010 and 2020 and investigate how these anomalies can be used in earthquake predictions, we examined the changes in the ionosphere before, during, and after the earthquakes. During my time as a support team member in this project, I had the opportunity to develop my skills in research, literature review, and reading academic papers.

HORIZON

In this project, we aim to research different telescope models, produce our own reflecting telescopes and deliver these produced telescopes to village schools. During my time as a member of the Optics Team in this project, I took part in polishing phase of grinded telescope glasses.
